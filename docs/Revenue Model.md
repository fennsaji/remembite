# Remembite – Revenue Model

---

## 1. Monetization Philosophy

Remembite is intelligence-gated, not quantity-gated.

Free users get unlimited access to all tracking and community data. The paywall sits in front of the AI layer — taste predictions, taste insights — which only becomes valuable after the user has built up a meaningful reaction history.

This design creates a natural, non-coercive upgrade moment: the user has already invested in the product, the data is already there, and upgrading reveals what that data means.

The goal is **habit first, revenue second**. A user who reacts to dishes every week is worth far more as a long-term subscriber than a user who paid ₹49 and churned in month 2.

## 4. Upgrade Triggers

The upgrade moment must feel like unlocking something the user already earned, not paying a toll.

### Primary Trigger: Taste Profile Completion

* Profile screen shows a completion bar: "Your taste profile is 80% complete"
* On completion: "Your taste profile is ready — see your food personality" → paywall
* This is the highest-intent moment in the user journey

### Secondary Trigger: Dish Detail AI Signal

* When a user opens a dish that would show an AI compatibility signal (confidence threshold met), but the user is not Pro:
* Show a blurred / locked state: "🔥 You'll probably love this — unlock AI predictions"
* This surfaces the value at the exact moment of decision — the highest relevance point

### Tertiary Trigger: Upgrade Screen from Profile

* "Upgrade to Pro" always visible on Profile screen
* Used by users who already understand the value and are ready to pay

### What is NOT a trigger

* A hard cap on dish count — creates frustration
* Pop-ups during core flows — interrupts the habit loop
* Time-limited trials — creates artificial urgency that feels manipulative

## 5. Retention Mechanics

Retention is driven by habit, not lock-in. The goal is to make Remembite a natural part of every restaurant visit.

**Core habit loop:**
Visit restaurant → open app → react to dishes → check community → order with confidence → repeat

**Mechanisms that reinforce the loop:**
* Visit timeline: rewarding to review, creates a personal dining journal
* "Your Top Bites" on the restaurant screen: immediate utility on every return visit
* Taste profile progress: creates a sense of an evolving personal record

**Pro-specific retention:**
* AI predictions improve as the user logs more reactions — the product gets more useful over time
* Cloud sync removes friction of switching devices
* Data export gives users confidence their history is portable (reduces exit anxiety)

---

## 6. Churn Prevention

### At the monthly plan level
Monthly churn is the primary risk at ₹49. A user who pauses dining out for a month may cancel.

Mitigations:
* Aggressive annual plan promotion at signup (₹399/year is the recommended default)
* Pre-cancellation reminder of what will be locked: "Your taste predictions will be paused. Your history is safe."
* Offer pause (1-month skip) as an alternative to cancel — reduces hard churn

### At the annual plan level
Annual churn happens at renewal. A user who has not engaged in months is unlikely to renew.

Mitigations:
* Renewal reminder 14 days before with a summary: "You reacted to 47 dishes this year across 18 restaurants"
* Re-engagement if activity drops: "You haven't logged a visit in a while — your profile is waiting"

## 7. Pricing Rationale

₹49/month is intentionally accessible. The bet is volume and habit over margin.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly price | ₹49 |
| Annual price | ₹399 |
| Monthly equivalent (annual) | ~₹33 |
| Annual discount vs monthly | ~32% |

At ₹49/month, Remembite sits in the same comfort zone as a single restaurant dish — easy to justify for someone who eats out regularly.

The risk of low pricing is low perceived value and high casual churn. This is mitigated by:
* Intelligence gating (AI predictions feel premium even at ₹49)
* Annual plan as default (reduces churn exposure)
* Habit loop that makes Pro feel essential once predictions are part of the ordering routine

If annual plan mix skews toward monthly, revisit pricing in Year 2.

## 8. Future Monetization Levers

These are not in scope for MVP but represent natural extensions of the data asset being built.

| Lever | Description | When |
|---|---|---|
| Restaurant analytics | Aggregated dish reaction data sold to restaurant operators ("your Dal Makhani scores lower on spice accuracy than competitors") | Year 2+ |
| B2B API | Dish intelligence layer licensed to dining apps, food delivery platforms | Year 3+ |
| Sponsored visibility | Restaurants pay for placement in "Nearby" (clearly labeled) | Year 2, only if community trust is high |
| Premium tier | Higher tier with social features, shared wishlists, dining companions | Year 2+ |

The community data and dish reaction graph is the core long-term asset. Every free user who logs a reaction is contributing to it.

---

## 9. Key Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Low conversion rate (<2%) | Improve upgrade trigger placement; test locked AI signal on dish detail |
| High monthly churn (>10%) | Push annual plan harder; add pause option |
| AI predictions feel inaccurate | Enforce confidence thresholds strictly; never show uncertain guesses |
| ₹49 too low to scale | Re-price to ₹99 in Year 2 if annual plan mix is poor and CAC rises |
| Free tier too generous | Monitor if active free users ever hit a natural pain point — can add soft cap later |
